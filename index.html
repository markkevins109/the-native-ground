Our Vision

A Living Repository of Rare Life

The present practices followed for cardamom cultivation are focusing on producing maximum quantity of cardamom ignoring other opportunities of generating income without compromising the biodiversity.


The project aims to create a unique Biodiversity Sanctuary—a living repository of rare, endangered, and functionally significant plants, trees, insects, birds, and animals.

Core Philosophy

  • Coexistence, not control
  • Quality, not quantity
  • Natural not Artificial

Alignment With

  • Traditional ecological wisdom
The Land

The 40–60 Acre Sanctuary

Strategically located adjacent to the 2024 disastrous landslide area, Native Ground is envisioned as a model for ecological resilience and restoration of Biodiversity.


At its highest point, a natural waterfall anchors the landscape, supported by abundant perennial water sources that enable efficient gravity-fed irrigation across the estate.

"Natural stream-fed water pockets provide essential drinking sources for wildlife, while gentle streams along both boundaries enhance both the ecological integrity and the visual appeal of this unique property."

Location & Strategy

Situated on the Vellarimala Mountain slopes. Serving as an ecological restoration buffer adjacent to the landslide-affected area.

Natural Features

Transforming the Cardamom estate to an evolving natural landscape where soil, shade, and native growth patterns seamlessly coexist with the Cardamom plants.

Sustainability & Impact

Verified Carbon Credits

Native Ground is developed as an emission-reduction project, committed to rigorous global standards. Our ecological restoration actively captures carbon, restoring balance to the climate while supporting native habitats.

  • Global Verification: In the process of registration with Verra and the Gold Standard.
  • Voluntary Carbon Markets: Credits generated for international companies (VCM).
  • Indian CCTS: Aligning with the upcoming Indian Carbon Credit Trading Scheme.
