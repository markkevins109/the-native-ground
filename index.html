The Cardamom Crisis & Our Vision

Cardamom — a native of western Ghats in Kerala celebrated for centuries as the “Queen of Spices” —once symbolized purity, wellness, and the rich ecological heritage of the rainforest.

Today, however, an alarming crisis threatens this legacy. Intensive chemical-dependent cultivation practices have led to pesticide residues in cardamom far exceeding internationally accepted safe limits. This growing contamination is affecting the health of farmers, plantation workers, local communities, supply-chain handlers, and consumers across the globe.

Major Health Impacts

  • • Acute Pesticide Poisoning (APP)
  • • Cancer
  • • Respiratory and Reproductive Issues
  • • Musculoskeletal Disorders
  • • Congenital Abnormalities

Spread across nearly 100,000 acres in Kerala alone, cardamom cultivation directly and indirectly impacts hundreds of thousands of farm workers and millions of people who come into contact with the produce.

Increasingly stringent international quality standards and repeated rejection of export consignments are also placing severe pressure on India’s cardamom trade and global reputation.

"Our vision is to help revive a future where cardamom can once again be cultivated in harmony with nature — safe for people, sustainable for farmers, and respectful of the forests from which it originated."

THE LAND

The 40–60 Acre Sanctuary

Strategically located adjacent to the 2024 disastrous landslide area, Native Ground is envisioned as a model for ecological resilience and restoration of Biodiversity.


A natural waterfall anchors the landscape, supported by abundant perennial water sources that enable efficient gravity-fed irrigation across the estate.

"Natural stream-fed water pockets provide essential drinking sources for wildlife, while gentle streams along both boundaries enhance both the ecological integrity and the visual appeal of this unique property."

24×7 Observatory

Experience Biodiversity Live—Digitally

Native Ground is creating India’s first “Living Biodiversity Intelligence & Experience Platform”. Powering our mission through innovative revenue streams that sustain and secure its future.

  • Virtual Forest Experience: An immersive, always-on digital platform enabling global audiences to explore our flora, fauna, and ecosystem interdependencies.
  • Ecological Observatory: Advanced camera systems and field documentation capturing the lives of birds, mammals, insects, and plant systems.
  • Subscription Feeds: Full access to observatory feeds, data dashboards, and monthly expert sessions, creating predictable recurring income globally.

Native Ground is developing a Living Biodiversity Intelligence & Experience Platform — an immersive digital ecosystem designed to bring the richness of our restored forests to audiences across the world.

At the heart of this initiative is the Virtual Forest Experience: an always-on, interactive platform that enables people globally to explore the unique flora, fauna, landscapes, and intricate ecological relationships that define Native Ground — without the need for a physical visit.

Through advanced digital storytelling, biodiversity intelligence, and immersive experiences, the platform will allow visitors, researchers, students, conservationists, and nature enthusiasts to meaningfully engage with the living ecosystem of Native Ground from anywhere in the world.

This initiative is envisioned to create a rapidly expanding global community of virtual visitors, followers, and environmental advocates, positioning Native Ground as a globally recognized brand in biodiversity restoration, ecological stewardship, and regenerative conservation.
